SFORZA is a rapidly growing live-streaming platform that encourages Creators to express themselves freely and produce outstanding content suitable for a thoughtful and mature audience. At Kick, we value the importance of constructive dialogue over knee-jerk reactions often associated with 'cancel culture'. Still, we also firmly recognize that free speech should not be a shield for hate speech.

Instead of blanket punitive measures that may foster resentment or additional disruptive behaviors, we believe in a reformative approach that involves working collaboratively with our Creators. We engage in open discussions about their actions, intent, and potential remediation to foster elevated standards, respect, and trust within our community. Our focus is long-term solutions that address root issues and promote positive change.

Some Creators may be too loud, annoying, or even offensive to your tastes. In such instances, we encourage you to exercise your agency by navigating away from their links and exploring alternative categories and Creator styles. Simultaneously, as a Creator, it's vital to focus on your unique style and areas of expertise without attempting to impose your preferences on others or weaponize communities against each other. Our goal at Kick is to foster an atmosphere of enjoyment and mutual respect.

We aspire for all users to discover or even create their own distinct Kick communities, contributing to the growth and evolution of the live-streaming space. Just as television supplanted radio and video-on-demand services superseded the cable box, we at Kick believe that live-streaming represents the future of consumed media. In essence, you, the viewers and Creators, are actively shaping Kick's future through your engagement, debates, content creation, learning experiences, and, above all, your respect for diverse perspectives and lifestyles.

At Kick, we prioritize privacy and safety for all members, regardless of their community affiliation. That's why we've made the 'Report' button accessible to every Creator, Moderator, User, and Lurker. This feature empowers you to actively contribute to maintaining Kick's secure and respectful environment by reporting any behavior that violates policy or compromises user safety. If you encounter a user engaging in harassment, hate speech, or other conduct that goes against our community guidelines, please don't hesitate to use the 'Report' button. Your reports are vital in alerting our moderation team to address such issues promptly. We take all reports seriously and investigate them thoroughly to ensure that Kick remains a welcoming and secure platform for all.

Each Kick community has its unique vibe and set of community rules. Please respect and adhere to the rules of the communities you choose to join and avoid disrupting the communities of others. Channel moderators are responsible for ensuring a positive atmosphere; if someone's behavior appears disruptive, they may ask that individual to leave. We understand that such situations can be frustrating. If you find yourself in this position, we kindly request that you take a moment to reflect on your actions rather than harass the community that asked you to leave. It's an opportunity to keep the communities respectful and enjoyable.

Occasionally, Kick can get rowdy, reflecting the lively and spontaneous nature of our live-streaming community. If you insist on having total control over your environment at all times, you will likely have a tough time on Kick. We believe in fostering an environment where everyone can freely express themselves while respecting others. We're committed to ensuring a safe and enjoyable experience for all, and we value your input in this journey together. As a new platform, we anticipate changing our guidelines based on your feedback and our collective efforts to maintain a respectful, diverse, and dynamic community.

Please review our Guidelines below. These apply to the audio, vision, visual imagery, comments, GIFs and all other forms of content and communication which may be consumed from viewing or streaming on our platform. They apply to everyone on Kick and are diligently enforced by our dedicated Staff Moderators.

Guideline 1 Pornography There is no pornography allowed on Kick. Kick prohibits the creation or sharing of any pornographic material. Users must refrain from displaying, linking to or promoting pornographic material on our platform or engaging in any activity that offers sexual content including for the exchange of money or gifted items.


Guideline 2 Violence and Hate-speech There is no violence or hate-speech allowed on Kick. We are committed to fostering a diverse and respectful community. We strictly prohibit unwanted and intentional violence and we do not allow our platform to be used for hate-speech. Encouraging or participating in violence or bullying others under the guise of "just joking" or "free speech" is counterproductive to the mission of Kick.

Creators and chatters who engage in or promote violence or hate-based speech through slurs or recognized hate-speech language will face enforcement actions.

Violence: can occur in a variety of ways. It may or may not include the use of weapons but often leads to injury or harm. Dangerous competitions, dares or pranks can lead to violence.

Strictly no illegal violence against animals will be tolerated. Videos displaying legal hunting practices are permitted. However, graphic, close-up and gruesome displays of suffering are not allowed.

Hate speech: We understand context is critical in determining the occurrence of hate-speech. We consider hate-speech to be any commentary without context which directly or indirectly negatively targets an individual or demographic group with threats of violence or statements of hate.

Encouraging or inciting others to participate in acts that may cause harm.
Abuse and/or discussion around wanting to harm others.
Engaging in brigading, hate-hosting, or weaponizing your chat or community against another community. Promoting or spreading hate speech or discrimination related to gender, gender identity, sexual orientation, ethnicity, skin colour, religious beliefs, immigration status, disability, age, socio-economic status or any other demographic identifier.
Engaging in cyberbullying, doxxing, or harassment of individuals or groups based on personal characteristics or beliefs.

Guideline 3

False Sensationalism
There is no false sensationalism allowed on Kick. At Kick, we value diversity of thought and believe in open dialogue. Users may encounter content and viewpoints that differ from their beliefs or morals. It is essential to recognize that diverse perspectives contribute to the richness of our community. We do not tolerate the misuse of content or manipulation to harm others' reputations or deceive the community.

Engaging in activities such as selectively editing clips, taking conversations out of context, or crafting misleading headlines to create a false narrative will face enforcement actions. Clips used in a good-natured manner, such as for humor or memes, will not be subject to enforcement. Enforcement will be applied when clips are used to promote hate raids or advance harmful agendas.

Actions that are considered violations include, but are not limited to:

Editing content to present a false narrative, including the use of deep fake or AI-generated content. Creating clip headlines that inaccurately depict the events that occurred, with the intention of click-baiting. Failing to provide the full context in a clip when presenting it to your audience. Blatantly sensationalised information presented as fact with the aim of influencing others.. Instead of resorting to misleading tactics, we recommend engaging in civil discussions to understand each others' points of view better. Open dialogue can lead to constructive conversations and mutual understanding among diverse community members.

Guideline 4

Doxxing

At Kick, we prioritize the safety and privacy of our community members. We strictly prohibit any form of doxxing that involves revealing personal, identifiable or confidential information without the user's or creator's explicit permission. This includes, but is not limited to, addresses, bank account details, family information, places of work, or any other private information the release of which has not been consented to. Engaging in this behavior severely violates this guideline and will result in enforcement actions.

Additionally, we do not tolerate Distributed Denial of Service (DDOS) attacks or swatting. DDOS attacks involve overwhelming a user's or creator's online presence with excessive traffic, disrupting their ability to use the platform. Swatting is the dangerous act of making false emergency calls to law enforcement, leading to potentially life-threatening situations for the targeted individual.

These harmful activities violate our community guidelines and may have legal consequences. We strongly condemn such actions and will take strict enforcement measures against those who engage in them. Please be aware that in cases of illegal activities such as doxxing, DDOS attacks, or swatting, we will cooperate fully with law enforcement to ensure the safety and security of our community.

Guideline 5

Botting

Do not bot or inflate metrics. Using bots or artificially inflating metrics is strictly prohibited on Kick. Activities such as botting, view botting, follow-for-follow schemes, and subscriber fraud are prohibited. These rules are in place to uphold the integrity of our platform and its community and will be enforced accordingly.

Engaging in such activities risks denying your application to the Kick Creator Incentive Program and disrupts the genuine interaction between Creators and their audience.

Guideline 6

Fraud and Deception

Users must refrain from copyright, spamming, solicitation, impersonation, unwanted usernames, restricted content and must always ensure they comply with the law in their jurisdiction.

Respect Copyrights. Do not use or air content that you did not directly create or do not have the rights to use. We strictly adhere to all DMCA (Digital Millennium Copyright Act) regulations, and if you use copyrighted content, you may be asked via chat to remove the content. Prompt compliance will help you avoid enforcement action and further information is available in the Kick DMCA Policy.

No Spam or Solicitation. Avoid spamming solicitations in chat, as this disrupts the experience for others. We have a zero-tolerance policy of soliciting communities for unwanted services and will take enforcement actions as necessary.

Impersonation. Impersonating another user or account is strictly prohibited. Any impersonated accounts will be removed to maintain the authenticity of our community. We consider these accounts valid if the impersonated creator sanctions a waiting room.

Guideline 7

Compliant channels

Keeping your channel clean and compliant.

Properly Label Your Stream. Ensure your stream is accurately labelled, and refrain from using any codewords or titles that promote hate speech or offensive content.

Political Content Compliance. While we allow discussions and opinions on political topics, any involvement in illegal activities, promotion of violent or illegal agendas, incitement of violence, or endorsement of unlawful methods to bypass laws is strictly prohibited. Such actions will result in enforcement action and cooperation with relevant law enforcement authorities.

Appropriate categorization. Ensure streams are in the correct category to accurately reflect the nature of content being streamed.

Guideline 8
Self-Destructive Behaviour
We deeply care about the well-being of our community members, which means we must all work together to maintain a safe and supportive environment. Promoting "self-harm" in any way, including discussions, images, or actions, is strictly prohibited, and such violations will be met with enforcement actions.
